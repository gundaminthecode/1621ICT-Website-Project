Shading with an airbrush.

This tutorial assumes you know the basics of building gunpla, prepping your kits for painting and the basics of airbrushing.

Things you'll need

  • A kit
  • An Airbrush
  • Paint

Pre-Shading

The kit being used in this demonstration is the Master Grade Gundam Spiegel. I will demonstrate how to paint a pre-shade with an airbrush using two different base colours.

Base Coat

Black Base

Blue Base

The base colour is very important when it comes to painting gunpla, because depending how transparent the top colour, the base colour may show through and can affect the tint that the colour has. When pre-shading, this is something we want to take advantage of. Generally, I tend to use a black base coat for most colours, but for colours such as yellow, the black base can heavily affect the final colour. In these examples, I will show the use of a black base for a dark blue, and a blue base for white.

Pre-Highlight

Pre-Highlighting is when you paint an are of white over the base coat, where you want the final colour to be brightest. White is most commonly used as it does not usually affect the colour in a negative way when being painted over.

Top Colour

The top colour is self-explanitory. As can be seen in the photos, the parts have slightly different coloured parts around the edges, this is thanks to the base coats we used. The parts also appear brighter in colour around the middle.
